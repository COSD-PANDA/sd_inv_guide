San Diego Data Handbook

Inventory

The purpose of the Data Inventory is to get a bird's eye view of data we have in the city and to assist stakeholders like the Mayor, Council, the public be able to identify data sets they consider high value for publication. It also has an added benefit of helping people find information much faster instead of relying on personal connections across departments. Lastly, it provides for a logical beginning to cataloguing which data we have and the likelihood of its release on the open data portal, taking into account all reasons it may not be able to be released.

It's important to remember that not all inventoried datasets will be released.

3 Main Steps

The Inventory Process is broken out into three main steps:

  • Identify Data Sources (Due April 1, 2015)
  • Identify Data Sets (Due May 1, 2015)
  • Complete Data Catalog (Due June 1, 2015)

Workload

Please rememeber that the inventory is designed to require minimal time from information coordinators. The deadlines are tight because this is designed to be a fairly quick process. Please note that it will be an ongoing process and the initial goal is to complete a citywide sweep of datasets that are readily identifiable. If during the course of this initial effort we neglect to include datasets in the inventory, we will include them later as we identify them. Please make your best effort to be comprehensive but do not allow the perfect to be the enemy of the good. If you feel like any of the steps will take an inordinate amount of your time (more than 2 to 3 hours a week), please email Maksim and let him know.
