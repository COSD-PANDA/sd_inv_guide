Kickoff Part 1

The kickoff was divided into two parts. First part we talked about the state of data in the city of San Diego, what is possible with open data, and what Data and Open Data really is.

Takeaways:

  • Opening Data Ties in directly with each of our Strategic Coals and allows us to monitor how we're doing at meeting them.
  • You were assigned as an information coordinator by your department Director. You will be working with Performance & Analytics and myself to create the data inventory and and to begin releasing data.
  • You may not be super technical, but you have an in-depth knowledge of your department operations, know the people to go to for answers and represent your department's/division's data profile.
  • Information Coordinators inventory department data sets, establish a plan and timeline for publishing them and serve as the key point of accountability for timelines and questions about data sets.
  • I will value your time and hope you will value mine.
  • The Open Data Policy passed in Council in December 2014 and is supported by Council and Mayor Faulconer.
  • Upcoming key dates for implementation:
    • March 31, 2015 - Guidelines for Data Inventory
    • June 1, 2015 - Inventory to be completed by departments
    • July 1, 2015 - Technical Guidelines (To address release protocol and PII)
    • July 1, 2015 - Initial Written Status Report
  • Today our data is
    • Closed
    • Misunderstood
    • Hard to find
    • Unknown
    • Scattered
  • That leads to
    • PRA Nightmares
    • Misinterpretation
    • Slowdowns
    • Re-Work
    • Lack of innovation
    • Frustration
  • Our data can be:
    • Accessible
    • Described
    • Reusable
    • Timely
    • Complete
    • Treated like asset
    • Centralized
    • Machine Readeable
  • Which would allow us to have:
    • Efficient PRA Responses
    • No Re-Work
    • Innovation
    • Empowerment
    • Efficiency
    • Engaged Citizens
    • Minimized Misinterpretation
    • Reliability
    • Data Usage for decisions
    • Cool apps!

  • In summation, "Open data is not just about putting spreadsheets on the internet. It means being deliberate and thoughtful about what is released, how it's released, and how it's described. Treating data like an asset and releasing it properly goes directly hand-in-hand with making sure that it's secure and mitigating opportunities for misuse"

  • What is Data?

    • A value or set of values representing a specific concept or concepts.
    • "Data is something you can take, and do something else with."
    • Please view from http://slides.com/sdcdo/odkickoff#/28 and a few slides down to see what is and is not data.
  • We MUST allow the capability to separate data from how it's shown in order for it to have value beyond what the visualization intended
  • The user may not share your opinion of what and how they want to visualize the same data. Important
  • "Data" can be separated from how it's displayed.
